Automated River Pollution Detection

A continental-scale monitoring system using satellite imagery and unsupervised machine learning to detect water pollution anomalies.

Context

Water pollution is escalating globally due to industrial discharge, agricultural runoff, and inadequate waste management. Traditional monitoring methods are slow, expensive, and spatially limited — often sampling only a few points along hundreds of kilometers of river.

This project explores whether satellite imagery combined with machine learning can automate pollution detection, identify anomalies in river systems, and ultimately support upstream source tracing.

The system prioritizes scale, reproducibility, and decision-making value over model novelty. It was designed for environmental agencies, conservation NGOs, and policy researchers who need actionable geospatial intelligence.

Technical Approach

Sentinel-2 imagery was selected for its global availability, 10–20m resolution, and rich spectral bands. Using Google Earth Engine, approximately 3,000 river locations were sampled globally and exported as 128×128 multispectral GeoTIFF patches.

Because large-scale ground truth pollution labels do not exist, the problem was framed as unsupervised anomaly detection.

Autoencoder Architecture

Hybrid CNN + dense layers for spatial pattern learning and spectral feature compression with enforced bottleneck constraint.

Ensemble Detection

Combined Isolation Forests, Elliptic Envelopes, and Z-score statistical methods to flag 10–15% of samples as anomalies.

Geospatial Outputs

Produced GeoJSON layers and pixel-level heatmaps suitable for mapping and policy analysis.

Iterative Refinement

Initial models learned pollution as "normal" when trained on mixed data—ensemble approach resolved this limitation.

Key Insights

  • Unsupervised methods work when labeled data is unavailable at scale
  • Multispectral data reveals patterns invisible to RGB imagery
  • Ensemble approaches compensate for single-model bias
  • Geospatial outputs must be immediately usable by non-technical stakeholders

Impact & Applications

The system enables rapid scanning of entire river networks, identifying hotspots for further investigation. It has potential applications in:

  • Environmental compliance monitoring
  • Disaster response (chemical spills, industrial accidents)
  • Long-term water quality trend analysis
  • Source identification for regulatory enforcement
